Internet of Things (IoT)

The application of IoT in agriculture can reduce and even become the right solution for various business problems in the agricultural sector. The accuracy of the Internet of Things can change the distribution of agricultural supplies and provide technology that makes agricultural logistics supplies smoother. The following is the practical application of the Internet of Things in agriculture:

  • Maximizing product

    • Weather, soil conditions, and market demand for certain crops are situations that affect the maximization of agricultural products. In making the right decisions, farmers need direct data about the weather conditions at that time. Wireless technology, mapping application GPS-based and also internet-based data storage can help a lot farmers for this.

  • Pest Control

    • The application of IoT in pest control and management issues can be applied into the form of a sensor network to monitor the number of pests can. Practically, if the sensor detects too many pests, the information can be sent to the pest control system automatically to take action. In some cases, this can replace the use of pesticides, so that the harvest can be healthier for consumption

  • Maximize production operations

    • Agricultural production activities include fertilizing, spraying pests and harvesting. All of these activities use special machines or equipment. By using Internet of Things, farmers can find the location of equipment in real time. They use this data to analyze and accurately determine effective location of the production operation area. This will certainly have a good impact on agricultural activity flow.
